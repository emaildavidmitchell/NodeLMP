
Ayad, Ragheb (1892 -1982)                                   203 words

Born into a Coptic family in a popular neighbourhood of Cairo, Ragheb Ayad
is a prominent member of a generation of Egyptian artists known as al-
ruwwd or pioneers.. He was among the first students of the newly
established School of Fine Arts in Cairo in 1908. Throughout his career, he
depicted scenes of rural and popular daily life in his oil paintings and
drawings. He created an original folklorist style inspired by the arts of
Ancient Egypt and traditional practices. Graduating in 1911, he taught at
the Great Coptic School before receiving a scholarship in 1925 to study at
the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Rome. Ayad was the initiator of the idea
of creating an Egyptian Academy in Rome. In 1930, he was appointed head of
the Decoration Department at the School of Applied Arts in Giza. He was
named director of the Free Section of the School of Fine Arts in Cairo in
1937. Between 1950 and 1955, he was the director of the Museum of Egyptian
Modern Art.
